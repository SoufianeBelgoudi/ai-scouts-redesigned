I focused on making the portal feel more intuitive and closer to how students actually use it.

One area I reworked was the Projects tab, which showcases work from previous cohorts. Right now, it feels a bit hard to quickly grasp what each project is about. I redesigned it using a card layout with visual previews so students can instantly see what others have built instead of just reading through text. It makes browsing past projects faster, more engaging, and honestly more inspiring.

I also wanted the experience to work naturally on mobile, since students don’t always use the portal on a laptop. Making the interface responsive allows them to interact with it in real situations, like quickly checking projects or confirming participation during sessions directly from their phone.

The design is inspired by platforms students already know, like GitHub, Figma Community, or Product Hunt. These interfaces make it easy to explore and discover content, and I wanted to bring that same feeling here.

Overall, the goal wasn’t just to make the portal look better, but to make it easier to understand, quicker to navigate, and more enjoyable to use.
